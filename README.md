## Is there a reason I chose to become a programmer?
My entrance into the coding world was also a matter of luck. During middle school, computers were a most sublime thing to me. The story begins during my high school years. At that time, I was a stellar student in my class (I was always at the top of the most outstanding students in my class, and I even took an excellent student test). I always strived to do well in all subjects, including informatics. And it brought me to the Pascal programming language (in my time, students in Vietnam still learned Pascal instead of C/C++). The first time I tried, I had difficulties understanding it, as I was weak in English (which most Vietnamese students had at that time). Fortunately, Pascal uses English syntax, but this does not discourage me. As part of my computer class, I studied syntax, data types, variable types, constants, functions, operators, conditions, loops, strings, etc... This enabled me to solve many complex Pascal problems. In this way, I was confident that I had the highest grade in the computer class. Despite the fact that I entered the IT industry through a stroke of luck, Pascal had a significant influence on my decision.
